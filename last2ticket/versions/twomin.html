Background

In April, The New Digital School invited companies to partner up with designers on UX projects. We got the chance to work with Last2Ticket, a ticketing platform that empowers event organizers (partners) and attendees with flexible e-ticketing tools.

Major findings from user research

During tests, users had points of low emotional state, triggered by interface issues that resulted in a slower buying process. These manifested in:

  • Users felt frustrated when they found out that they had to login/register to buy a ticket.
  • Users felt surprised with the number of input fields on the registration page. They described them as being "too many". They also felt uncomfortable when the widget prompted them to share their phone numbers.
  • Users felt anxious about the popup screens and the pre-payment screen.

Improving the buying process

We explored the widget ourselves, working collaboratively with the client to get context about business and user needs. From there we conducted user testing with 6 users, were we absorbed learnings that would help us in defining the problem.

We proposed to solve the problems on the login/registration flow, by giving priority to primary user actions.

Improving the experience for users and event promoters

We went further and researched how we could improve the refund process. From guerilla research on the streets and contact with the Last2Ticket team, we validated the need to work on such topic.

We proposed to solve the problems with the refund process by reselling refunded tickets, increasing the revenue per ticket.

Learnings

  • The process should be flexible, not set in stone – we felt the need to adapt when things turned out differently than expected.
  • Broadening the perspective brings new things to the table – we got findings from guerilla testing on the streets of Porto, asking different unexpected questions.
  • Fidelity is a marathon, not a race – moving too fast on the solution fidelity can lead to bad decisions.
  • The delivery is an experience – we didn’t present just screens to the client, but involved them in our process.
